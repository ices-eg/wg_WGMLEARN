# 2026 WGMLEARN meeting

When: November, 2026
Where: ICES HQ, Copenhagen

## Planned session topics

### Exploring the Machine Learning Frontier

This topic will examine where marine science currently lags behind developments in AI and where future research efforts should focus. The session will contrast mature fields (e.g., image classification) with developing needs (e.g., acoustics). We will explore emerging AI methodologies (e.g. PINNs, GNNs , and the integration of mechanistic and data-driven models.)

### From Pilot Projects to Operational Deployment

In many cases, AI research ends with pilot systems and proofs-of-concept, but fail to become operational tools. This session will explore the obstacles to operationalizing AI, moving beyond technical hurdles to address soft factors. Topics include organizational shifts, psychological barriers (fear of change, distrust of "black box" AI, job security concerns), regulatory obstacles, and changing our mindsets from crafts to industry.

### Building AI-Ready Marine Data Ecosystems

Data scientists often complain that almost all their time and effort is spent on cleaning up data, and high-quality, interoperable, and accessible data remain fundamental to successful AI applications. This session will examine how to handle rapidly growing data collection and analytics while maintaining quality and reproducibility. Discussions will cover automated data flows, standardized formats for surveys, cloud systems, and why current FAIR standards often struggle with practical adoption.

### Developing Cross-disciplinary Capacity

Successful AI applications require a competence continuum covering ecology, oceanography, computer science, statistics, engineering, and industry. This session challenges the naive approach of simply grouping experts together, focusing instead on teaching ecologists about ML, computer scientists about ecology, and supporting "AI-poor" regions or domains with limited expertise.

### Responsible and Effective Use of Large Language Models

Large Language Models are rapidly becoming part of scientific workflows, from report writing and coding to knowledge extraction and decision-making. This session will examine practical applications, limitations, governance, ethical considerations, and regulatory frameworks like the EU AI Act. We will explore grassroots adoption models, such as informal "AI Coffee" forums and internal institution surveys.
